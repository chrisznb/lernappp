## 1. KOTTER'S 8 CHANGE MANAGEMENT STEPS (10 Points)

### The 8 Steps Overview
**Mnemonic:** "UK For Cash Every Generation Shall Continue"
- **U**rgency
- **K**oalition
- **F**orm Vision
- **C**ommunicate
- **E**nable Action
- **G**enerate Wins
- **S**ustain
- **C**onsolidate/Anchor

---

### Step 1: Create a Sense of Urgency
**What:** Create urgency for change
**How:**
- Show numbers (competition, market trends)
- Challenge the status quo
- Bring external reality into the company

**Exam Example (Call Center ‚Üí Open Office):**
"Show the numbers: Modern companies have 30% higher productivity with Open Office. Competitors have already converted."

---

### Step 2: Build a Guiding Coalition
**What:** Build a leadership coalition
**How:**
- Recruit influential people
- Different areas/hierarchies
- Group credibility important

**Exam Example:**
"Win over 2-3 Team Leaders who are convinced of Open Office and form the change group with them."

---

### Step 3: Form a Strategic Vision
**What:** Develop a clear vision
**How:**
- Vision must be understandable and motivating
- Strategy for achievement
- Picture of the future

**Exam Example:**
"Vision: 'Modern, collaborative work environment that promotes innovation and facilitates teamwork.'"

---

### Step 4: Communicate the Vision / Enlist a Volunteer Army
**What:** Communicate the vision
**How:**
- Repeatedly and clearly
- Use all channels
- Walk the talk (be a role model)

**Exam Example:**
"Team meetings, emails, bulletin board. Show mock-ups. Q&A sessions."

---

### Step 5: Enable Action by Removing Barriers
**What:** Remove obstacles
**How:**
- Adapt systems/structures
- Address resistance
- Provide resources

**Exam Example:**
"Noise-canceling headsets, focus rooms, height-adjustable desks, training in collaboration tools."

---

### Step 6: Generate Short-Term Wins
**What:** Celebrate quick successes
**How:**
- Plan and make early successes visible
- Maintain motivation
- Quick wins

**Exam Example:**
"After 1 month: Celebrate first team collaboration, share positive feedback quotes, show productivity increases."

---

### Step 7: Sustain Acceleration / Consolidate Gains
**What:** Use successes for further changes
**How:**
- Don't stop too early
- Continuous improvement
- Use momentum

**Exam Example:**
"Introduce flexible working hours, hot-desking, further optimizations."

---

### Step 8: Institute Change / Anchor in Culture
**What:** Anchor in culture
**How:**
- Make new behaviors the norm
- Show connection between behavior and success
- "New Normal"

**Exam Example:**
"After 3 months: Integrate collaborative working into onboarding, tell success stories."

## 2. ORGANIZATION ARCHETYPES (10 Points)

### Functional Organization
**Definition:** Structure by functions (Marketing, Finance, Production, HR)

**Advantages:**
- ‚úì High specialization
- ‚úì Economies of scale
- ‚úì Clear career paths
- ‚úì Efficient resource utilization

**Disadvantages:**
- ‚úó Silo thinking
- ‚úó Slow decisions
- ‚úó Poor coordination between functions

**When to use?**
- Single-product companies
- Stability > Flexibility
- Example: "Windshield wiper business - other products hardly play a role"

---

### Divisional Organization
**Definition:** Structure by products/regions/customers

**Advantages:**
- ‚úì Clear product/market responsibility
- ‚úì Fast decisions
- ‚úì Customer focus
- ‚úì Flexibility

**Disadvantages:**
- ‚úó Duplication of resources (expensive!)
- ‚úó No economies of scale
- ‚úó Competition between divisions

**When to use?**
- Multi-product companies
- Different markets/regions
- Example: "International consumer goods - regions should have key decision-making power"

---

### Matrix Organization
**Definition:** Combination of functional and divisional

**Advantages:**
- ‚úì Flexible
- ‚úì Good resource utilization
- ‚úì Specialization + product focus

**Disadvantages:**
- ‚úó Complexity
- ‚úó Conflicts between managers
- ‚úó Violation of "Unity of Command"

**When to use?**
- Complex projects
- Temporary teams
- Both expertise and product focus needed

---

### Exam Case: Consumer Goods + Dietary Supplements

**Situation:**
- Consumer Goods (Home Care + Body Care)
- Acquired: Dietary Supplements
- Old: Functional

**Recommendation: DIVISIONAL**

**PRO Argument:**
"The three product categories have different customers, distribution channels, and requirements. A divisional structure enables product-specific marketing and specialized distribution strategies."

**CONTRA Argument:**
"Duplication of functions like marketing and distribution leads to higher costs and loses synergies in purchasing and production."

## 3. GENDER EQUALITY - AMMERMAN & GROYSBERG (10 Points)

### The 7 Talent Management Processes

**Mnemonic:** "Hiring People Creates Professional Skills That Retain"
1. **H**iring
2. **P**erformance Evaluation
3. **C**ompensation
4. **P**romotion
5. **S**ponsorship
6. **T**alent Development
7. **R**etention

---

### 1. HIRING
**Core Problem:** "Are we fishing in the same pond as everyone else?"

**Measures:**
- Gender-neutral job postings
- Diverse recruitment channels (not just elite universities)
- Structured interviews
- Blind recruiting
- Critically review job requirements

---

### 2. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION
**Core Problem:** "Are our evaluations truly objective?"

**Measures:**
- Clear, measurable criteria
- 360-degree feedback
- Calibration meetings
- Bias training
- Focus feedback on behavior

---

### 3. COMPENSATION
**Core Problem:** "Are we paying fairly?"

**Measures:**
- Pay equity audits
- Salary transparency
- Clear salary bands
- Don't ask about previous salary
- Proactively offer fair salaries

---

### 4. PROMOTION
**Core Problem:** "Are we promoting based on potential or track record?"

**Problem:** Men on potential, women on track record

**Measures:**
- Transparent criteria
- Active talent spotting
- Succession planning
- "Ready now, ready soon, ready later"

---

### 5. SPONSORSHIP
**Core Problem:** "Who is advocating for women behind closed doors?"

**Important:** Sponsorship ‚â† Mentorship
- Sponsor opens doors
- Mentor gives advice

**Measures:**
- Formal sponsorship programs
- Assign senior leaders as sponsors
- Access to informal networks

---

### 6. TALENT DEVELOPMENT
**Core Problem:** "Are we providing equal development opportunities?"

**Measures:**
- Fair distribution of high-visibility projects
- Rotation through departments
- Distribute "office housework" fairly
- International assignments for all

---

### 7. RETENTION
**Core Problem:** "Why are women leaving?"

**Measures:**
- Evaluate exit interviews (gender-specific)
- Flexible work models for ALL
- Career paths after parental leave
- Inclusive culture
- Make role models visible

## 4. PERSONNEL PLANNING & FTE (8 Points)

### FTE Calculation Formula

```
FTE = Workload (hours) / Available hours per person
```

**Components:**
- Workload = Number of tasks √ó Time per task
- Available = Working days √ó Hours/day √ó Utilization

### Exam Example: QuickDrop Logistics

**Given:**
- 4 million packages/year
- 4 min/package
- 80% utilization

**Assumptions (ALWAYS state!):**
- 250 working days/year
- 8 hours/day = 2,000 hours/year

**Calculation:**
```
Workload: 4,000,000 √ó 4 min = 16,000,000 min = 266,667 hours
Available: 2,000 √ó 0.8 = 1,600 hours
FTE = 266,667 / 1,600 = 167 employees
```

---

### Seasonality Management

**Situation:**
- December: +25%
- July/August: -10%

**Measure 1: Flexible Workforce**
"Fixed-term seasonal workers in December. Partner with staffing agencies. Early recruitment from October."

**Measure 2: Variable Working Hours**
"Working time accounts: Build up overtime in December, reduce in summer. 4-day week in July/August."

**Other Options:**
- Student helpers
- Vacation planning
- Outsourcing peak volumes

## 6. LEADERSHIP MODELS

### Tuckman's Team Development Phases

| Phase | Relationship Level | Leader Role | Hersey/Blanchard Style |
|-------|-------------------|-------------|------------------------|
| **FORMING** | Polite, uncertain | **Directive** - Define goals & roles | S1: TELLING |
| **STORMING** | Conflicts, power struggles | **Coaching** - Manage conflicts | S2: SELLING |
| **NORMING** | Cohesion, norms | **Participative** - Decide together | S3: PARTICIPATING |
| **PERFORMING** | High performance | **Delegating** - Independent | S4: DELEGATING |

---

### Hersey & Blanchard Maturity Levels

| Level | Competence | Commitment | Style | Description |
|-------|-----------|------------|-------|-------------|
| **M1** | Low | High | S1: TELLING | "Can't but willing" - Beginner |
| **M2** | Some | Low | S2: SELLING | "Can't and unwilling" - Disillusioned |
| **M3** | High | Variable | S3: PARTICIPATING | "Can but unsure" - Competent but hesitant |
| **M4** | High | High | S4: DELEGATING | "Can and willing" - Expert |

**Exam Examples:**
- M2: "After initial success, employee realizes it can take very long..."
- M3: "Employee excellently prepared but doubts the night before..."

---

### Span of Control

**Definition:** Number of employees directly reporting

**Calculation:**
```
Team Leads = Agents / Span
Dept Heads = Team Leads / Span
Total = Team Leads + Dept Heads
```

**Exam Example: Call Center (400 Agents)**
- Old: 10 Agents/Lead, 8 Leads/Head ‚Üí 40 + 5 = 45
- New: 8 Agents/Lead, 5 Leads/Head ‚Üí 50 + 10 = 60
- **Change: +15 Managers**

**LARGER Span possible when:**
- Simple, routine tasks
- Experienced employees
- Clear processes
- Geographically concentrated

**SMALLER Span needed when:**
- Complex tasks
- New employees
- Geographically distributed
- Much coordination

**Exam Examples for SMALLER:**
- "Flexible working hours ‚Üí never in office at same time"
- "New claims rules ‚Üí constantly new approaches"
- "Personal communication in large meetings"

## 7. COGNITIVE BIASES

### Availability Bias
**Definition:** Overestimating easily remembered events

**Exam Example:**
"Fear of vaccinations because of famous actor with side effects"

---

### Halo Effect
**Definition:** One characteristic overshadows everything else

**Exam Example:**
"Cristiano Ronaldo for 200M for healthy living ‚Üí Soccer ‚â† Health ambassador"

---

### Confirmation Bias
**Definition:** Only perceiving confirming information

**Exam Example:**
"Google shows search results by click probability ‚Üí Filter bubble"

---

### Fundamental Attribution Error
**Definition:** Others' behavior on personality, own on situation

**Example:**
- He late: "He is unpunctual"
- I late: "There was traffic"

## 8. CROSS-CULTURAL MANAGEMENT

### Confrontation-Avoidant vs Confrontational

**Avoidant (Asia):**
- Dissent as negative
- "Saving face"
- Indirect communication

**Confrontational (Germany, Netherlands):**
- Open disagreement productive
- Devil's advocate

---

### Linear Time vs Flexible Time

**Linear (Germany, USA):**
- Sequential
- Punctuality
- "Time is money"

**Flexible (Latin America, Africa):**
- Multitasking
- Smooth transitions
- Relationships > Schedules

---

### High-Context vs Low-Context

**High-Context (Japan, China):**
- Implicit communication
- "Reading between the lines"
- Theory ‚Üí Examples

**Low-Context (USA, Germany):**
- Explicit, direct
- "Say what you mean"
- Opinion ‚Üí Theory

## 9. TEAMWORK

### Ringelmann Effect (Social Loafing)
**Definition:** The larger the group, the less effort per person

**Measures:**
- ‚úì Involvement on cognitive level
- ‚úì Individual performance measurable
- ‚úì Clear roles
- ‚úó NOT: Uniform qualification
- ‚úó NOT: Low performance targets

---

### Kirkpatrick's 4 Levels

| Level | What is measured? | Method |
|-------|------------------|---------|
| **1: REACTION** | Satisfaction | Feedback forms |
| **2: LEARNING** | Knowledge, skills | Tests, assessments |
| **3: BEHAVIOR** | Application at workplace | Observation, 360¬∞ |
| **4: RESULTS** | Business impact | ROI, KPIs |

**Important:**
- Written exams NOT for Behavior (Level 3)
- Results easily measurable (numbers!)

**Explanation:**
- **Level 1** measures participant satisfaction and perceived usefulness
- **Level 2** measures knowledge acquisition through tests (NOT behavior change!)
- **Level 3** measures actual behavior change in the workplace (cannot be measured by written exams)
- **Level 4** measures business impact through KPIs like quality, productivity, sales

## 11. PERSONNEL SELECTION MISTAKES

### First Order Mistake (Alpha Error / Type I)
**Definition:** Hiring UNSUITABLE candidate (False Positive)

**Exam Example:**
"Professor good in interview but can't teach ‚Üí catastrophic evaluations"

**Consequences:**
- Poor performance
- Costs
- Difficult to correct

---

### Second Order Mistake (Beta Error / Type II)
**Definition:** Rejecting SUITABLE candidate (False Negative)

**Exam Example:**
"Candidate nervous in presentation ‚Üí rejected ‚Üí becomes star professor at another university"

**Consequences:**
- Lost talent
- Opportunity costs
- Less obvious
